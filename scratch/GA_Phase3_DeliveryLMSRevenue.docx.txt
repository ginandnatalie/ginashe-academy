Different learner contexts require different channels — a rural smallholder farmer cannot attend a Johannesburg campus weekly, and a working professional in Sandton does not need a mobile learning unit. The four channels work together to reach every learner segment in South Africa and the broader continent.The four delivery channelsCommunity campusesPhysical hubs in townships, peri-urban, and rural areasShared equipment, fibre connectivity, facilitator-led cohort model. Prioritised for hands-on schools — Applied Trades, Health Sciences, Agriculture. Each campus operates as a learning community, not a building.Online-first LMSMobile-first platform reaching every SA provinceSelf-paced and cohort courses delivered via the Ginashe Academy LMS. Designed for low-bandwidth South African networks. Facilitator office hours twice weekly. Used heavily by Digital Systems, FinTech, and Creative Media.Employer partnershipsB-BBEE skills spend cohortsCorporates fund cohorts for their staff or community pipeline. Customised curriculum, on-site or hybrid delivery, bursary and stipend models. Rotates through rural municipalities on a weekly schedule. Brings the campus to the learner. Critical for Agriculture, Energy, and Digital Literacy.Learner support infrastructureDelivery channels alone are not enough. South African learners face structural barriers — cost of data, lack of devices, transport costs, family responsibilities, and economic precarity. Ginashe Academy addresses these directly:Stipend modelRemoving the financial penalty of learningUnemployed learners receive a stipend of R1,500 to R3,000 per month during contact learning. Funded through SETA discretionary grants, the National Skills Fund, and employer B-BBEE levy contributions. Without stipends, the most vulnerable learners cannot afford to attend even free courses — they need to eat and travel during the learning period.Device programmeRefurbished laptops on loanLearners who do not own a personal computer receive a refurbished laptop on loan for the duration of their course. 75% of the device cost is subsidised by corporate partners as part of B-BBEE enterprise development spend. At course completion, the learner can purchase the device for R500 or return it for the next cohort.Data bundlesRemoving the bandwidth barrierOnline learners receive a 5 GB monthly data bundle for the duration of their course. Ginashe Academy negotiates zero-rated access to ginashe.academy with MTN, Vodacom, and Telkom — meaning learners can use the LMS without consuming their personal data allowance. WhatsApp integration for assignment submission ensures even data-poor learners can participate.Section 3 — Accreditation PathwayAccreditation is the foundation of credibility, funding access, and qualification recognition. It is the primary channel for the online-first delivery model and the secondary channel for hybrid courses. The architecture is designed for South African network conditions — mobile-first, offline-capable, low-bandwidth, with WhatsApp integration for the most data-poor learners.Five-layer platform architectureLearner layerMobile-first LMS (Moodle + custom PWA)Course catalogue · Video lessons · Interactive labs · Quizzes · Assignment submission · Progress dashboard · Peer forums · Certificate download · Offline content syncFacilitator layerFacilitator portalCohort management · Attendance register · Assessment marking · Learner progress alerts · Resource library · Facilitator community · CPD tracker · 1-on-1 office hours schedulerAdmin layerAcademy management systemEnrolment & intake · SETA reporting automation · Employer billing · Grant claim processing · Analytics & outcomes · Certificate registry · SAQA submission · Compliance dashboardAI layerGinashe AI tutor (Claude API)24/7 question answering scoped to course context · Auto-grading of short-answer questions · Personalised learning path recommendations · Facilitator feedback drafts · Content gap detectionInfrastructureAWS af-south-1 Cape Town — all data in SAEC2 autoscaling · RDS PostgreSQL Multi-AZ · S3 video CDN · CloudFront · KMS encryption (POPIA) · CloudTrail audit · Disaster recovery · 99.9% SLA · POPIA-compliant by designLow-bandwidth design principlesThe LMS is built for South African network conditions — patchy coverage, expensive data, intermittent connectivity. Three design principles dominate every technical decision:Offline-firstPWA with service workerThe LMS is built as a Progressive Web App with a service worker that caches lessons locally. Learners download content when they have connectivity, then study offline. The LMS syncs assignments and progress automatically when the learner reconnects. Designed to function on 2G connections at minimum.Video compressionAdaptive bitrate streamingAll video content uses adaptive bitrate streaming. Mobile users default to 360p; the player only requests higher quality on stable Wi-Fi. Every video has a transcript for text-based study. Audio-only mode lets learners study during commutes when video data costs are prohibitive.WhatsApp integrationReaching the data-poor learnerA custom WhatsApp bot lets learners submit assignments, take quizzes, ask the AI tutor questions, and receive facilitator notifications via WhatsApp. For learners whose data plans only cover WhatsApp (the majority of low-income SA users), this is the difference between participation and exclusion.Section 5 — Facilitator PipelineThe quality of an academy is the quality of its facilitators.
