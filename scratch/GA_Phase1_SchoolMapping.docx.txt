The Academy began as the School of Digital Systems (SDS), focused on cloud computing through the Cloud Launchpad programme. With the acquisition of the ginashe.academy domain and the strategic decision to expand beyond the digital niche, SDS becomes one of ten schools within a broader institution serving the most urgent skill demands of South Africa and the African continent.This document — Phase 1 of 4 — presents the school mapping. It sets out which schools will be established, why each is strategically important for the South African and continental context, and the order of priority for launch.Subsequent documents in the Strategic Blueprint cover:Phase 2 — Curriculum Blueprint: detailed course structures, NQF levels, and delivery models for all 80+ courses.Phase 3 — Delivery, LMS & Revenue: delivery channels, accreditation roadmap, LMS architecture, facilitator training, and the revenue model.Phase 4 — Governance, Brand & Web: board structure, brand identity system, website architecture, partnership framework, and legal structure.Selection criteriaThe 10 schools were selected and ranked using four criteria:Skills gap urgency — how acute is the SA shortage in this skill area?Employment yield — does graduating from this school produce immediate employment or entrepreneurial pathways?Continental relevance — does the skill transfer beyond SA into the broader African market?Delivery feasibility — can Ginashe Academy realistically deliver this school at scale within 24 months?Tier classificationEach school is classified into one of three tiers:Flagship (5 schools) — immediate SA need, launching in Year 1.Strategic (4 schools) — 2 to 5 year growth horizon, launching in Year 1 to 2.Emerging (1 school) — continental scale opportunity, launching in Year 2.Section 2 — The Ten SchoolsThe schools are presented in priority order. School #1 — Digital Systems (SDS) — is already operational. Schools #2 to #5 are flagship schools to be launched in Year 1. Schools #6 to #9 are strategic schools for Year 1 to 2 launch.
