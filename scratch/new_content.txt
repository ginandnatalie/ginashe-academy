Descriptions Foundation — SETA NQF Level 3–4 Designed for school leavers, first-job seekers, and individuals entering the digital economy with no prior specialist knowledge. No formal qualifications beyond Grade 9–11 are required. Courses at this level provide practical, job-ready skills in 8–12 weeks, and are ideal for TVET partnerships, YES programme participants, and government-funded learnership pipelines. Associate — SETA NQF Level 5 Targets early-career professionals, career changers, and upskilling workers in the first 1–3 years of their career. Entry requires a Foundation certificate or equivalent work experience. Courses run 10–16 weeks and prepare learners for vendor associate certifications (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud, CompTIA, Microsoft). Professional — SETA NQF Level 6 For mid-career specialists, senior practitioners, and team leads seeking to deepen expertise or formalise experience. Courses run 12–20 weeks, include capstone projects, and align with professional certifications (AWS Solutions Architect, CISSP, Azure Expert, etc.). Learners should have 2+ years of industry experience or hold an Associate-level certificate. Enterprise — SETA NQF Level 7–8 Executive and senior leadership programmes. Targets CTOs, CISOs, CDOs, senior engineers, and strategic leaders responsible for digital transformation at organisational scale. Delivered in cohort or modular format over 16–24 weeks. Includes executive workshops, industry mentors, and board-presentation practicals. Regulatory & Accreditation Framework South African Qualifications Authority (SAQA) All GDA qualifications will be registered on the National Qualifications Framework (NQF) via SAQA. Each course maps to formal NQF unit standards with clearly stated exit-level outcomes, assessment criteria, and credit values. This enables learners to accumulate credits toward formal qualifications recognised by employers and government. Quality Council for Trades and Occupations (QCTO) Foundation, Associate, and Professional courses will be submitted to QCTO for occupational qualification registration. This places GDA graduates on recognised occupational pathways relevant to ICT, digital media, and business services — sectors identified by the South African Government's Operation Vulindlela and Digital Economy Strategy. SETA Alignment Courses are mapped to unit standards under MICT SETA (Media, Information and Communication Technology) and SERVICES SETA where applicable. This enables employer Skills Development Levies (SDL) to fund learner bursaries, and qualifies GDA as a provider for W&RSETA, BANKSETA, and other sector SETAs. Pan-African Recognition Curriculum content and assessments are designed to be relevant across Sub-Saharan African markets. The academy will pursue mutual recognition agreements with equivalent bodies in SADC, ECOWAS, and EAC member states, supporting learner mobility and cross-border employment opportunities in the growing African digital economy. SADC Qualifications Framework (SADCQF) alignment pursued from Year 2 ECOWAS digital skills framework compatibility built into all Foundation and Associate curricula Partnership pathway with African Union Commission's Digital Transformation Strategy Content localised for South Africa with adaptation guides for Nigeria, Kenya, Ghana, and Zimbabwe Course Descriptions — All 28 Courses Each course below is mapped to a SETA NQF level and designed to stack progressively within its track. About Ginashe Digital Academy Ginashe Digital Academy (GDA) is a purpose-built learning institution under the Ginashe Group, headquartered in Johannesburg, South Africa. Its mandate is to accelerate digital transformation across South Africa and the broader African continent by equipping learners — from school leavers to C-suite executives — with job-ready digital skills. All programmes are designed for alignment with the South African Qualifications Authority (SAQA) National Qualifications Framework (NQF), SETA unit standards, and QCTO qualifications — positioning GDA for formal accreditation and government recognition. The academy is equally structured to meet the skills development requirements of continental frameworks such as those promoted under the African Union's Digital Transformation Strategy. 2. Curriculum Philosophy GDA programmes are built on three foundational principles: Earn-while-you-learn pathways — all courses include practical, project-based assessments that map directly to workplace tasks, ensuring learners are employment-ready from day one. Stackable qualifications — each course is a building block. Learners enter at the Foundation level and progress to Enterprise level within a track, or move laterally across tracks as their careers evolve. African context — case studies, data sets, and capstone projects are drawn from South African and pan-African business realities, ensuring relevance for the local market. 3.
