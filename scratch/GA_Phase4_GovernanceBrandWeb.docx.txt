This is the cleanest configuration for the early years and offers four advantages over running the Academy as a separate, independent entity:Brand and credibility transfer — the Group's existing relationships, vendor contracts, banking infrastructure, and credibility transfer to the subsidiary instantly. A separate independent entity must rebuild all of this from scratch.Tax efficiency on early-year losses — early-stage Academy losses can be group-relief'd against Ginashe Group profits in the same financial year. As a separate independent entity, those losses sit unused on the books for years before becoming valuable.Operational simplicity — shared services (IT infrastructure, finance, HR, legal counsel) flow naturally between parent and subsidiary without elaborate management-fee paperwork or transfer-pricing complexity. Auditors and SARS understand and accept this structure routinely.Future optionality — if the Academy later requires NPC conversion (when SETA grants and donor money become significant) or external investment, separating a subsidiary is far simpler than restructuring an independent entity. The subsidiary can also operate alongside a future sister NPC for the bursary or grant arm.Service-level agreement between parent and subsidiaryRequired even within the same groupA formal SLA between Ginashe Group and Ginashe Academy documents the management services provided (IT, finance, HR), the fees charged (transparent, market-rate), and the delivery commitments. This is the right structure for the early years — operationally simple, commercially flexible, and well-understood by SARS, banks, and partners. Maintains the founder's direct decision-making authority and avoids the governance complexity of an NPC at a stage where the institution is still finding its operating rhythm.Recommended near-term configurationThe recommended structure for the next 18 to 36 months is for Ginashe Academy (Pty) Ltd to operate as a wholly-owned subsidiary of Ginashe Group (Pty) Ltd. This is a minor structural change from independent Pty Ltd to subsidiary Pty Ltd, but it unlocks meaningful advantages.ParentGinashe Group (Pty) Ltd — holding companyThe existing holding company. Owns the Ginashe brand and intellectual property. Provides operational management services to the subsidiary on a transparent management fee basis. Maintains the broader Ginashe Group corporate identity and existing commercial relationships.SubGinashe Academy (Pty) Ltd — operating subsidiaryWholly-owned subsidiary of Ginashe Group. Operates all 10 schools. Generates revenue from learner fees, corporate B-BBEE cohorts, government contracts, and (where eligible as a Pty Ltd) selected SETA programmes. Maintains its own bank accounts, audit, and statutory returns.SLAGroup Service AgreementA formal service-level agreement between Ginashe Group and Ginashe Academy documents the management services provided (IT, finance, HR, legal), the fees charged at market rate, and the delivery commitments. Required by SARS for transfer-pricing compliance and by SETAs as evidence of operational independence at curriculum level.Why subsidiary is better than independent for nowBrand and credibility transfer — the Group's existing relationships, vendor contracts, banking infrastructure, and credibility transfer to the subsidiary instantly. A separate independent entity must rebuild all of this from scratch.Tax efficiency on early-year losses — early-stage Academy losses can be group-relief'd against Ginashe Group profits in the same financial year. Auditors and SARS understand and accept this structure routinely.Future optionality — converting to NPC or creating a sister NPC is significantly easier from a subsidiary structure than from an independent Pty Ltd. The subsidiary can also accept outside investment in the future without restructuring the entire group.Future-state options — when and why to consider changeThe current Pty Ltd structure is right for now. Three future scenarios may make sense to consider at the appropriate moment, none of them urgent:Future option A — Convert Ginashe Academy to NPCTrigger: when SETA grants and donor money exceed 30% of revenueA full conversion of Ginashe Academy from Pty Ltd to NPC unlocks SETA discretionary grant eligibility, Section 18A donor receipts, PBO tax exemption, and B-BBEE CSI/ED contribution recognition. Worth considering when grant and donor revenue would exceed 30% of total revenue and the loss of commercial flexibility is justified by the funding gain.Future option B — Create a sister NPC alongside the Pty LtdTrigger: when bursary endowment or donor funding reaches material scaleA separate Ginashe Academy Foundation NPC operates alongside the Pty Ltd. The NPC handles bursaries, donor funding, and SETA grants. The Pty Ltd handles commercial training, corporate cohorts, and content licensing. Best of both worlds — preserves commercial flexibility while unlocking grant funding. Typically established when capital donor commitments reach R5 to R10 million plus.Annual compliance — current Pty Ltd obligationsAs a Pty Ltd, Ginashe Academy maintains an annual compliance calendar with five recurring requirements.
