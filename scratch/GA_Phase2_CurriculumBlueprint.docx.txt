It is the working detail behind Phase 1's school mapping — what learners will actually study, for how long, in what mode, and toward which job outcomes.Document structureThe document is organised by school. Each school occupies its own section and follows the same structure:School banner — name, abbreviation, mission, and rank.Summary block — learning pathways, cross-school synergies, and the school's overall course profile.Course cards — one per course, showing name, NQF level, duration, delivery mode, modules, graduate outcome, and entry requirement.Course delivery modesThree delivery modes are used across the curriculum:In-person — physical campus or workshop attendance, used for hands-on practical courses (trades, hardware, clinical practice).Online — fully digital delivery via the Ginashe Academy LMS, used for theory-heavy and individually-paced courses.Hybrid — mixed delivery, usually online theory plus in-person practical sessions or workshops.Course modes — cohort versus self-pacedCourses operate in one of two scheduling modes:Cohort — fixed start and end dates, group of learners moves through the course together with the same facilitator. Higher completion rates, stronger peer support.Self-paced — learner enrols any time and progresses at their own pace. Useful for working professionals, supported by asynchronous facilitator office hours.Workshop — short, intensive courses (4–10 weeks) usually delivered as a single block. Used for skill-specific upskilling.NQF levels used in this curriculumAll courses are aligned to the South African NQF (National Qualifications Framework). Levels in this curriculum range from L2 to L6:NQF Level 2–3 — foundational. Suitable for learners with Grade 9–10. Equivalent to lower secondary school competence.NQF Level 4 — basic occupational. Equivalent to matric or N3. Most school courses sit here.NQF Level 5 — intermediate occupational. Equivalent to first-year tertiary or N4–N5. Many specialist courses sit here.NQF Level 6 — advanced occupational. Equivalent to advanced certificate or diploma.
